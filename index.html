Avoid Election Misinformation

Seek Reliable Resources

Fact-checking is important to identify facts from misinformation. It is advisable to check in multiple articles or websites about the topic to further check if the information is credible.

Educate Yourself

Make sure to learn more about the candidate and their platforms from reliable sources. Take time to check if their platforms will be significant in the community. We must also be aware of our biases because it might affect our perception.

Verify Information

These are the websites you can Factcheck and Verify Information:

  • FactCheck.org
  • The Associated Press (AP) Fact Check

Research Candidates

Learn about the candidates' positions, policies, and track records. Understand where they stand on issues that matter most to you and your community.

Tips for Researching Candidates:

  • Review their official campaign websites
  • Check their voting records and past positions
  • Look for third-party fact-checking sources
  • Attend candidate forums and debates
  • Research their financial disclosures

Understand Voting Laws

Know your rights and familiarize yourself with voting regulations in your area.

Important Voting Laws to Know:

  • Voter ID requirements
  • Registration deadlines
  • Early voting options
  • Absentee voting rules
  • Provisional ballot procedures

Make Your Vote Count

Vote with your values and consider the issues, policies, and character of the candidates.

How to Make Your Vote Count:

  • Vote based on issues that matter to you
  • Consider the candidate's track record
  • Look at their policy proposals
  • Consider their leadership qualities
  • Think about their vision for the future

Engage Respectfully

Democracy thrives on dialogue. Engage with others respectfully, even when you disagree.

Principles of Respectful Engagement:

  • Listen actively to others
  • Stay open to different perspectives
  • Respectfully challenge misinformation
  • Focus on issues, not personalities
  • Seek common ground

Identify Your Non-Negotiables

Establish clear deal-breakers that would prevent you from supporting a candidate:

  • History of human rights violations
  • Corruption charges or involvement in scandals
  • Dishonesty or lack of transparency

Scrutinize the Candidates

Evaluate candidates based on their actions:

  • Track Record: What have they actually accomplished in office or their profession?
  • Consistency: Have they been true to their principles, or do they change stances for political gain?
  • Leadership Style: Are they accountable, open to dialogue, and solution-oriented?

Evaluate Character & Public Service

  • How do they treat their opponents, critics, and the public?
  • Do they unite or divide people?
  • Have they demonstrated true public service, or are they driven by self-interest?

Research Policies & Compare Platforms

  • Read their official platforms and past speeches
  • Compare their stances on key issues with other candidates
  • Consider how their policies align with your values and the country's needs
